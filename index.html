Mission

The Design Intelligence Lab advances design strategy and decision-making through evidence-based, systematic methods.

We integrate design evaluation and audit, design IP and governance, and AI-enabled design thinking to develop practical frameworks and tools for organizations and society.

Through both academic research and industry collaborations, we pursue responsible innovation that accounts for design value, risk, and rights.

Research Focus

Our research integrates strategic thinking, systematic evaluation, governance frameworks, and emerging AI capabilities to advance the field of design intelligence. We develop practical tools and methods that organizations can implement to make better design decisions.

01

Design Management & Strategy

Strategic frameworks for design decision-making in complex organizational contexts, bridging business objectives with design innovation through systematic approaches.

  • Design strategy development and implementation methodologies
  • Design portfolio management and optimization
  • Cross-functional design leadership models
  • Strategic design metrics and KPI frameworks
02

Design Evaluation & Audit

Evidence-based methods for assessing design quality, impact, and value across products, services, and systems using both qualitative and quantitative approaches.

  • Design quality assessment frameworks and criteria
  • Design impact measurement and ROI analysis
  • Design process audit and maturity models
  • Comparative design evaluation methodologies
03

Design IP, Governance & Ethics

Management of design intellectual property rights, ethical frameworks, and governance structures for protecting design assets while ensuring responsible innovation in the AI era.

  • Design IP strategy and protection mechanisms
  • AI design ethics and responsible innovation frameworks
  • Design governance policies and digital rights management
  • Design asset valuation and licensing models
04

AI √ó Design Thinking

Integration of artificial intelligence into design processes, exploring AI-augmented creativity, ethical considerations, and the future of human-AI collaboration in design practice.

  • AI-enabled design tools and methodologies
  • Machine learning for design decision support
  • Generative AI applications and ethical implications
  • Human-AI collaboration frameworks in design

Director

EK (Eunkyong) Baek, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor

Professor Baek distinctively combines design management academia with intellectual property expertise. Before joining IDAS, Hongik University, she directed De Montfort University's MA Design Management program (UK), taught at Munich University of Applied Sciences (Germany) and KAIST (Korea). During her time in Germany, she worked on design related Intellectual Property rights at Weickmann, a leading European IP law firm.

This unique convergence of expertise positions the Design Intelligence Lab at a critical intersection: where design strategy meets intellectual property protection and AI-enabled innovation. With a Ph.D. in Design Management and two decades bridging design, business, and law, Dr. Baek develops practical frameworks for organizations navigating design value creation and protection in the digital age.

Collaboration Opportunities

The Design Intelligence Lab welcomes collaborations that advance design intelligence research and practice. We bridge academic rigor with practical application to develop frameworks and tools that enhance organizational design capabilities.

We're particularly interested in connecting with partners who share our commitment to evidence-based methods, responsible innovation, and the integration of AI into design processes:

  • Organizations seeking design intelligence frameworks for strategic decision-making and value assessment
  • Researchers exploring design management, AI-augmented design thinking, or design IP governance
  • Companies interested in design evaluation methodologies and audit systems
  • Graduate students pursuing research in design intelligence and related interdisciplinary fields
  • Public sector organizations developing design policy and innovation frameworks
